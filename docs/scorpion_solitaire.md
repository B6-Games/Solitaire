## Rules of Scorpion Solitaire

### üÉè Objective

The goal of Scorpion Solitaire is to build **four complete sequences**, each from **King down to Ace** in the same suit.  
Once a sequence is complete, it‚Äôs removed from the tableau. You win when all four suits are completed.

---

### üé¥ Setup

- Played with **one standard 52-card deck**.  
- Deal **49 cards** face up into **7 tableau columns**:
  - The first four columns each get **7 cards**, with the **top three cards face down**.  
  - The last three columns each get **7 cards**, all **face up**.  
- The remaining **3 cards** are placed aside as the **stock pile**, face down.

Only the top cards of the tableau piles are playable at first; as face-down cards are uncovered, they become available.

### üèÜ Winning the Game

You win when all cards are arranged into **four descending King-to-Ace sequences** of the same suit.  
These sequences are usually automatically removed or visually separated when complete.

If you reach a state where no moves are possible, the game ends - though careful planning can prevent deadlocks.

## Strategy Tips

- **Expose face-down cards early.** The faster you uncover hidden cards, the more flexible your moves become.  
- **Focus on suit continuity.** Only same-suit sequences can move together; avoid mixing suits unless necessary.  
- **Keep columns open.** Empty spaces are extremely valuable for moving Kings or long sequences.  
- **Plan before dealing stock cards.** Once dealt, the stock cards can block columns if not used wisely.  
- **Don‚Äôt rush to move Kings.** Only move them when it strategically helps free up new plays.

## Variations

- **Easy Scorpion:** Uses fewer face-down cards or allows building regardless of suit.  
- **Simple Simon:** A close variant where all cards start face up, making it more about logic than luck.  
- **Spider Solitaire:** A related game played with two decks and more complex movement rules.
