## Rules of Forty Thieves Solitaire

### üÉè Objective

Build all **eight foundation piles** by suit, in ascending order from **Ace to King**.  
Once all cards are moved to the foundations, you win.

---

### üé¥ Setup

- Played with **two full decks (104 cards)**.  
- Deal **ten tableau columns**, each with **four face-up cards**.  
- The remaining cards form the **stock pile**, face down.  
- The top of the stock can be turned up one card at a time into the **waste pile**.  
- The **foundations** start empty and are built by suit in ascending order.

## üß† Strategy Tips for Forty Thieves Solitaire

- **Focus on freeing columns early.** Open spaces are crucial for maneuvering cards.  
- **Play Aces and low cards quickly.** They unlock foundation progress and free up the tableau.  
- **Avoid blocking key cards.** Moving cards too early can block sequences later on.  
- **Keep suits aligned.** You can only build within the same suit - mixing suits limits your options.  
- **Use the waste pile carefully.** Always check if a card can be moved to a foundation before covering it with a new draw.

## Variations

- **Little Forty Thieves:** A simplified version with fewer tableau columns.  
- **Double Klondike:** Also uses two decks but allows more flexible building rules.  
- **Streets and Alleys:** Similar layout but all cards are visible from the start.  
- **Forty and Eight:** Allows building regardless of suit, making it easier for beginners.
